The Seven Elemental Realms

🔥
The Ashveil Dominion
Fire Region · The Northern Reaches
"Here the mountains breathe smoke and the rivers run with molten stone — a land that burns not out of rage, but because it knows no other way to live."
Terrain
Volcanic mountain chains, lava fields, scorched plains, obsidian deserts
Climate
Perpetually hot, ash-choked skies, rare sulfurous rain, eternal ember-glow at night
Capital
Cinderholm — a city carved into the caldera of a dormant supervolcano
People
The Emberi — bronze-skinned, fiercely proud, masters of forging and flame-calling
The Ashveil Dominion stretches across the northern reaches where the earth's crust is thinnest and the world's inner heat bleeds freely upward. Jagged volcanic peaks pierce ash-grey clouds in every direction, and between them lie seas of cooled lava cracked like ancient skin. Travelers speak of the Flame Roads — ancient paths carved through hardened magma flows, glowing faintly underfoot with residual heat. The Emberi people believe fire is not a weapon but a language, and their Pyroglyphic script is literally burned into stone using focused flame. Their warriors carry cindersteel blades forged in volcano hearts, and their healers use controlled flame-touch to cauterize wounds and burn away infection.

The Tidesong Expanse
Water Region · The Southern Reaches
"The south is never still. Even in silence the currents speak, and those who learn to listen find that the sea remembers everything."
Terrain
Vast ocean archipelago, coral labyrinths, drowned ruins, tidal marshlands
Climate
Humid, rain-heavy, shifting monsoons, bioluminescent nights, warm currents
Capital
Pearlspire — a city built on interconnected reef platforms above the deep
People
The Marathi — lithe, long-lived, born navigators with a gift for tidal prophecy
The Tidesong Expanse is less a land than a world of water interrupted by islands. Thousands of isles ranging from jungle-crowned mountain peaks to barely-surfaced reefs form a living maze that shifts with the tides. The Marathi have mapped every current, every hidden channel — knowledge passed through generations in their oral tradition of Wavesong, a form of singing-navigation where melodies encode routes across vast distances. Beneath the surface lie the Drowned Cities, ancient civilizations consumed by rising seas millennia ago, now haunted by tidal spirits the Marathi call Riptongues. Their warriors fight using fluid-form combat, moving like water — absorbing force, redirecting strikes, never resisting directly.
ArchipelagicPropheticSeafarersPatientMemory-keepers

The Rootstone Heartlands
Earth Region · The Central Expanse
"Stand on the Heartlands long enough and you begin to feel it — a slow pulse beneath your feet, old and steady, like the world breathing in its sleep."
Terrain
Ancient mountain ranges, deep canyon networks, vast fertile plains, crystal cavern systems
Climate
Four true seasons, rich soil, deep aquifers, protective stone ridges blocking harsh weather
Capital
Ironveil — built into a mountain that is also the world's largest geode
People
The Terrath — stocky, enduring, stonemind scholars and agricultural engineers
The Rootstone Heartlands occupy the world's center — the oldest land, where the first mountains rose and where the earth's memory is deepest. The landscape is one of dramatic contrasts: soaring ridgelines fall into lush valleys, and beneath rolling plains lie the Crystal Warrens, a vast network of caverns lined with naturally formed gemstones that pulse with low elemental energy. The Terrath build downward as much as upward — their cities are as deep as they are tall, with the most ancient and sacred chambers buried in living bedrock. They practice Stonemind, a discipline of patience and geological awareness that allows trained practitioners to sense mineral veins, underground water, and even fault lines from great distances.
EnduringScholarlyDeep-buildersSeasonalStone-readers

The Galereach Wilds
Wind Region · The Eastern Frontier
"Nothing is fixed in the east. The dunes shift overnight, the forests lean in one direction, and even the sky seems to be passing through on its way to somewhere else."
Terrain
Open steppes, towering wind-sculpted mesas, singing dune seas, cliff-face forests
Climate
Constant winds, dramatic pressure fronts, rare stillness treated as omen, wide sky
Capital
Vortessa — a city of towers and sky-bridges built to channel rather than resist the wind
People
The Zephrin — lean, swift-moving, nomadic-hearted, their language shaped by wind patterns
The Galereach Wilds are defined by motion. Prevailing winds sweep east to west in great spiral currents, carving the mesas into fantastical spires and giving the dune seas their restless geometry. The Zephrin have built their culture around this impermanence — even their permanent structures are designed to be disassembled and relocated, and their philosophy holds that attachment to place is a kind of spiritual weakness. Their Wind-Scribes are the world's most sought-after weather-readers and messengers, using kite-like gliders to travel vast distances in days. The rare windless moments known as Dead Calms are considered deeply ominous — silence, to the Zephrin, means something is listening.
NomadicSwiftSky-readersUnattachedMessage-bearers

The Stormvault Coast
Lightning Region · The Western Shore
"The west does not ask permission. The storms arrive unannounced, the thunder speaks before the lightning, and the people have learned to think just as fast."
Terrain
Storm-battered coastline, charged highlands, glasslands fused by ancient lightning strikes
Climate
Perpetual electrical storms inland, violent coastal squalls, electromagnetically charged air
Capital
Arcveil — a city of copper spires and underground conduit networks harvesting storm energy
People
The Volthari — sharp-minded, intensely fast thinkers, innovators, crackling with restless energy
The Stormvault Coast is the most electrically active place in the known world. Towering cumulonimbus formations rotate above the western highlands in near-constant rotation, and the Glasslands — vast plains of sand fused into translucent sheets by millennia of lightning strikes — are considered sacred ground. The Volthari have turned their dangerous homeland into a power source, developing intricate networks of Stormconduits — copper-and-obsidian channels that capture lightning and distribute its energy throughout their cities. Their engineers are the world's most technically brilliant, and their warriors use charge-arms: weapons that store and release electrical energy in concentrated bursts. Speed of thought is the culture's highest virtue.

The Luminarch Isles
Light Region · The Floating Islands
"To reach the Luminarch Isles you must first believe they are there — for their light bends perception, and only those with clear purpose can find the ascent."
Terrain
Suspended stone islands, prismatic crystal formations, cascading light-falls, sky meadows
Climate
Perpetually golden-lit, above the cloud layer, temperature steady, breezes carry warm luminescence
Capital
Solhaven — built around a crystalline spire that refracts sunlight into rainbow pillars
People
The Aurelith — pale, luminous-eyed, truth-seeking, long-lived contemplatives and judges
The Luminarch Isles drift above the cloud layer, tethered to the earth only by ancient chains of hardened light the Aurelith call Solbindings. From below, they appear as glowing shapes in the sky — unreachable, almost mythical. From above, they are a paradise of impossible beauty: meadows of silver grass, forests of crystalline trees that chime in the wind, and waterfalls of liquid light that cascade over island edges and dissolve before reaching the earth below. The Aurelith are the world's foremost judges and keepers of truth — their Luminary Courts are considered unimpeachable, as the light magic of their home makes deception nearly impossible within island borders. Their greatest power, Truthcast, forces clarity of perception on all within its radius.

The Hollowed Below
Dark Region · The Underworld
"Those who descend into the Hollowed Below rarely return the same. The dark there is not the absence of light — it is a presence of its own, and it has a long memory."
Terrain
Vast sub-surface caverns, void-black lakes, petrified forests, shadow-matter plains
Climate
No sun, no seasons, cold-warm cycling from deep geothermal vents, absolute darkness outside settlements
Capital
Noctaveil — carved into a cavern so vast its ceiling is mistaken for sky
People
The Umbrath — adaptive, shadow-sighted, secretive, masterful in illusion and hidden knowledge
The Hollowed Below exists beneath the surface of the known world — a realm of staggering scale hidden from sunlight. Its caverns dwarf surface nations, and the Voidlakes that fill the lowest reaches contain creatures and substances found nowhere else. The Umbrath have thrived here for uncounted generations, developing senses beyond sight and a cultural philosophy centered on the idea that darkness is not emptiness but potential — the state before all creation. Their Shadowweavers can manipulate shade and darkness like a physical substance, constructing structures from solidified shadow and hiding armies within patches of absolute dark. They are the world's foremost spies, scholars of forbidden knowledge, and keepers of secrets that the surface world would prefer forgotten.
